Introducing Jatayu

May 2026

We are Jatayu, and we are building AI agents that are truly accessible to everyone.

The current wave of AI agents is powerful but largely inaccessible. Agents today assume technical fluency, unimpaired vision, standard input devices, and fluency in English. They are built for a narrow slice of the world's population. We think that is a problem worth solving.

Accessibility is not an afterthought or a compliance checkbox. It is a design principle. When AI agents can be used by a person who is blind, or deaf, or who navigates the world with a motor impairment, or who speaks a language that the tech industry has historically ignored, those agents are simply better for everyone.

We are building the infrastructure and interfaces that make AI agents work for people across the full spectrum of abilities, languages, and contexts. This includes how agents communicate, how they accept input, how they present output, and how they adapt to the person using them. Not wrapping existing agents with accommodations, but designing agent architectures where accessibility is structural.

AI agents are at an inflection point. They are moving from demos to daily use, from chat windows to real-world tasks. The decisions made now about how agents interact with people will define the defaults for years. We want those defaults to be inclusive. The founding team brings together experience in AI systems, assistive technology, and human-computer interaction. We have seen what happens when accessibility is bolted on late. We are here to make sure that does not happen with agents.

We are in stealth and heads-down building. A formal announcement with details on our work, our team, and what we are releasing will come soon. If this mission resonates with you, we would love to hear from you.
